About Us

For more than three generations, Hollow Creek Farm has been a cherished destination where families can slow down, explore the countryside, and celebrate the beauty of each season. Owned by the Calloway family since 1962, the farm began as a small vegetable operation founded by Bill and Margaret Calloway and has since grown into one of the region's most beloved agritainment destinations. In 2008, the farm was passed to their daughter, Dana, and her husband, Rob, who now work alongside their children, Tyler, Meg, and Sam, to continue the family tradition. Spanning 140 acres, Hollow Creek Farm features working fields, a restored 1890s barn, a sunflower field, a corn maze, a pick-your-own pumpkin patch, a small orchard, and a covered pavilion for events. Today, visitors return year after year for fresh produce, farm store goods, seasonal activities, and the welcoming atmosphere that makes Hollow Creek Farm a place where memories are made and life slows down.
